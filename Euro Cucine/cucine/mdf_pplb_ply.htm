MDF PPLB & PLY

MDF & PB are    reconstituted wood substitutes & can be manufactured from timber having lower log diameter, as low as 5cm - which at any stretch could be only used for fire wood. Hence, 2.25MT of Wood is required to manufacture 1MT of MDF a conversion that eleminates wastage.

MDF/PB is an engineered solution to the natural timber & is available year round.

MDF/PB has a homogeneous construction hence results in a an uniform physical & mechanical properties across the length & breadth of the panel.

MDF/PB has a multi-dimensional bonding amongst the fibre/chips with higher active bonding surfaces - higher in case of MDF. This results in higher Internal Bond & the strength of the product.

MDF/PB has a ultra-smooth paint / polish ready surface & this ensure the ease of application & as a matter of fact MDF/PB has been used with a transperent lacquered surface which is not possible with plywood/BB.

With the Pre-laminated MDF/PB the user has the option of multiple shades/decors in Pastel & Wood Grain best suited to the asthetices of the interior decore.

MDF can be profiled, carved, moulded, routed & PB to certain extent in edge moulding & surface routing.

During the Defibration Process the natural timber is converted to fibres & in this process the borers are destroy if they did exist in the nascent timber.

Plywood is manufactured from veneers that are pealed off mature timber Hence logs of higher dia are must resulting into high level of wastage. Whereas Block Board has Solid wooden Frame & batons with veneer based faces pressed on either surface.

With the natural timber resources becoming scarce plywood & Block Board availability is under pressure .        

Plywood/BB doesn’t have homogeneous construction & has density variations resulting into variable physical & mechanical properties. 

Plywood/BB has a uni-dimensional bonding - that is surface to surface.

Such a bond is weak & the prime reason for Plywood & BB feathering at the edges. It has lower Internal Bond.

Plywood/BB has a rough wood textured surface despite the sanding. The surface needs to be made paint/polish ready or even for the matter of fixing MICA or Veneer. 

Incase of Plywood/BB the Pre-lamination cannot be done at all. Hence the user has finish the furniture/panels in the conventional method using Paint/Polish/MICA. However, one has to smoothen the surface.

Plywood/BB cannot be carved or routed or profiled. It shall always have a straight profile & zero workability.

Borers reside in wood hence is one of its prime issue commonly know as GHUN.
